ORIGIN

The bayou keeps its secrets. In the murky waters of Louisiana's deepest swamps, something survived that shouldn't have. A child, abandoned or lost—the truth died with whoever left him there—raised not by humans, but by the ancient rhythm of the wetlands.

He believed he hatched from a gator nest. The great reptiles didn't kill him; they ignored him. To a child's mind, that meant family. He learned to move like them—silent, patient, deadly. He learned to hunt without being seen, to strike without warning, to disappear into water and shadow alike.

For years, he was the swamp's ghost. Hunters whispered of something watching them. Poachers vanished. The locals called it cursed ground. They weren't wrong.

Then Splinter found him. The old rat saw something in those amber eyes—something human, buried deep beneath feral instinct. He brought the wild one north, to live among his sons. But integration is slow. Trust is slower.

Now he watches from the shadows of the sewer, learning their ways, keeping his distance. The turtles train. He observes. They fight together. He waits. Somewhere between the swamp and the city, between animal and man, Gator searches for what he truly is.
